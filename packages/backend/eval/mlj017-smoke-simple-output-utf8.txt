## Dust Control and Silica Exposure Prevention

-   **Dust Control Measures:**
    -   Utilize wetting, HEPA-Vac, or green dust methods (p. 22).
    -   Implement and adhere to the approved Dust Control Plan (p. 22, 23).
    -   Stop work if visible dust is observed outside the barricaded area and correct the source (p. 23).
    -   Use wet methods to control airborne dust emissions or vacuuming methods as per 1926.1153 Table 1 (p. 22).
    -   Wet methods (green dust or water) used to sweep during housekeeping (p. 24).
    -   IDA (Indeco Dust Abatement) System uses high-pressure water spray to prevent dust (p. 43).

-   **Saw Cutting Specifics:**
    -   Water hose connected to the demo saw prior to operation (p. 23).
    -   Ensure direct water supply or pressurized water tank available for saw cutting (p. 23).
    -   Only trained and authorized workers permitted to use saw cutting equipment (p. 22).

-   **Respiratory Protection:**
    -   Employees will use N95 dust masks or ┬╜-face respirators (p. 22).
    -   Follow the approved Dust Control Plan for respiratory protection (p. 23).
    -   All workers conducting demolition must be medically cleared and fit-tested for respiratory protection (p. 22).
    -   Respiratory training [1926.103] and protection in accordance with 1926.1153 Table 1 (p. 22).

-   **Silica Exposure Prevention:**
    -   Follow OSHA Silica Fact Sheet Attachment #1-2 (p. 21).
    -   IDA system protects operators against exposure to microparticles of crystalline silica (p. 43).
    -   IDA system updated to comply with latest OSHA directives (p. 43).

-   **Containment & Housekeeping:**
    -   Fire retardant tarp and/or fire-rated plywood to contain debris (p. 22).
    -   Housekeeping conducted daily throughout the work shift (p. 24).
    -   Remove all unused materials from the job site daily (p. 24).
